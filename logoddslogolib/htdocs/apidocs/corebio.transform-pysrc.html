109      """ Mask low complexity regions in protein sequences. 
110       
111      Uses the method of Seg [1] by Wootton & Federhen [2] to divide a sequence    
112      into regions of high and low complexity. The sequence is divided into 
113      overlapping windows. Low complexity windows either have a sequence entropy 
114      less than the trigger complexity, or have an entropy less than the extension     
115      complexity and neighbor other low-complexity windows. The sequence within    
116      a low complexity region is replaced with the mask character (default 'X'),  
117      and the masked alphabetic sequence is returned. 
118       
119      The default parameters, width=12, trigger=1.8, extension=2.0, mask='X' are 
120      suitable for masking protein sequences before a database search. The  
121      standard default seg parameters are width=12, trigger=2.2, extension=2.5 
122       
123      Arguments: 
124          Seq seq         -- An alphabetic sequence 
125          int width       -- Window width 
126          float trigger   -- Entropy in bits between 0 and 4.3.. ( =log_2(20) ) 
127          float extension -- Entropy in bits between 0 and 4.3.. ( =log_2(20) ) 
128          char mask       -- The mask character (default: 'X')  
129      Returns : 
130          Seq         -- A masked alphabetic sequence 
131      Raises : 
132          ValueError  -- On invalid arguments 
133      Refs: 
134          [1] seg man page:  
135              http://bioportal.weizmann.ac.il/education/materials/gcg/seg.html 
136          [2] Wootton & Federhen (Computers and Chemistry 17; 149-163, (1993))  
137      Authors: 
138          GEC 2005 
139      Future : 
140          - Optional mask character. 
141          - Option to lower case masked symbols. 
142          - Remove arbitary restriction to protein.
